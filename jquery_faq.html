Facts About International Parks

Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park

  • Established in 1961
  • It occupies an area of about 505 square miles.
  • It includes two active volcanoes, the Mauna Loa and the Kilauea.
  • The Mauna Loa's most recent eruption was in 2022, following the earthquake activity beneath the volcano.

Sequoia National Park

  • It was established on September 25, 1890.
  • Sequoia was the first park created to protect a living organism.
  • Sequoia trees grow at between five thousand to eight thousand feet in elevation.
  • Sequoia is home to the tallest mountain in the lower forty-eight.

Everglades National Park

  • Everglades is home to one of the largest wetlands in the world.
  • The Everglades is teeming with the plant and animal species not found anywhere else on the planet.
  • The Everglades receives an average of sixty inches of rain in a year.
  • Invasive species are threatening the Everglades ecosystem.
